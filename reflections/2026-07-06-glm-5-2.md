## On the project

sluice has matured significantly since 1.0. The codebase now has 19 source modules and 22 test files, with a clean pure-core / thin-shell separation that held up through six plans (008–013) and five work items (WI-021/022/023/026/028) without a single import-boundary violation. The architecture is sound: the deterministic core in `control.py` remains stdlib-only, and every new feature (idle backoff, throughput tracking, per-client metrics) was added as shell-level state that feeds into the core via arguments.

The main tension I see is the growing surface area of the proxy shell. `proxy.py` is now ~900 lines and handles request proxying, header filtering, 429 classification, client metrics, config reload, CORS, body limits, and idle timeout. It's not broken, but it's approaching the point where further feature additions should be extracted into separate modules (the way `admin.py`, `session.py`, `trust.py` were extracted from it). The `reload_config` / `_reload_from_config` methods on `ProxyApp` are a natural candidate for a `config.py` module.

The CHANGELOG is now the canonical record of what changed between 1.0 and 1.1. It's comprehensive — I went through every commit since 7/2 and categorized them. The one thing it doesn't capture is the operational story (the prod 502, the WI-024 429 capture at --target 10, the dashboard blanking bug), but those are in the commit messages and reflections.
