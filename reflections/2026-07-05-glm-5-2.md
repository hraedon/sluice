## On the project

sluice is in good shape. The pure-core / thin-shell split holds up under scrutiny — `control.py`
imports nothing outside stdlib, and the import-boundary test enforces it. Plan 013 (honest
Retry-After) is a genuinely elegant piece of work: the reason-dispatch in `retry_after_seconds()`
gives an honest deadline for boxed/breaker states and a jittered pressure estimate for saturation,
rather than a flat lie. The Plan 012 cookie/CSRF surface is the part I'd least like to debug at
3am — it's correct, but the interaction of `sluice_session` stripping, CSRF same-origin checks,
and the admin-token fallback has enough moving parts that the test coverage there is load-bearing.

The one structural smell is that `reflections/` is tracked at the repo root and accumulating
session artifacts that arguably shouldn't live in the public repo (they reference `ghcr.io/hraedon/...`,
which is fine as public identity, but the directory itself has no place in AGENTS.md's described
layout). Not blocking; worth a decision.

## On the work done

Confident in: the nits (mechanical, verified), the redaction (grep-clean, YAML valid), the
identifier gate (faithful port of a proven script + the sibling's test suite, all 15 new tests
pass), and the 3.14 matrix addition (gpo-lens already gates on the same trio, 3.14 has been GA
since Oct 2025). The squash-merge cleanly collapsed the five docs-fixup commits — including the
one that added then redacted a hostname in `client-configuration.md` — into a single clean commit,
which is exactly the history-cleaning the squash was for.

Less confident / worth a second look: the **dependabot auto-merge workflow**. The mechanism
(`gh pr merge --auto --squash`) is correct, but its safety depends entirely on repo settings I
can't verify from here — specifically branch protection on `main` requiring the `ci` status
checks. Without that, auto-merge fires immediately on PR open with no safety valve (the glm
reviewer caught this; I reworded the comment to make the prerequisite explicit). The operator
needs to enable both "Allow auto-merge" AND set required checks, or this is either a no-op or
worse-than-manual.

The `retry_after_seconds()` docstring fix is the change I'd most want re-read: I rewrote the
reason table to move `RETRY_AFTER_SHORT` into the breaker entry (where it's actually returned as
the `opened_at`-unknown fallback) and reframed the draining/not_leader line as a caller-handled
note rather than a method return case. Both reviewers agreed the old docstring was wrong; verify
my rewrite matches your mental model.
